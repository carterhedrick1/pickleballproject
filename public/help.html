Game Modes Explained

IN or OUT offers two distinct registration modes that give you completely different levels of control over who joins your game. Choose the right one based on your event type and how you want to manage players.

‚ö°

First Come First Serve Mode

Transparent, instant joining with automatic waitlist management

üîÑ How Players Join

  1. 1
    Players click your game link and see all current players listed by name - complete transparency
  2. 2
    If spots are available, players instantly join and get SMS confirmation with their player number
  3. 3
    When full, new players go to waitlist with position shown (e.g., "You are #3 on the waitlist")
  4. 4
    Cancellations automatically promote the next person with instant SMS notification

üëÄ What Players See

  • Full player list: Names of everyone currently confirmed
  • Real-time availability: "2 spots available" or "Game is full - join waitlist"
  • Instant feedback: Immediate confirmation or waitlist position
  • Transparent process: Can see exactly who's playing

‚öôÔ∏è How You Manage It

Your management interface will typically show:

  • Confirmed Players: Full roster (4/4 players)
  • Waitlist: Small overflow (1-2 people waiting)
  • Your role: Monitor and handle exceptions
  • System does: All automatic promotions and notifications

See detailed management examples in the "Managing Your Game" section ‚Üí

üì± Organizer Notifications

You can choose to receive SMS alerts for:

  • Someone joins: "Mike Chen just joined your game. 1 spot remaining."
  • Game becomes full: "Your game is now FULL! All 4 spots taken."
  • Someone cancels: "Mike Chen cancelled. 1 spot now available."
  • One spot left: "Only 1 spot remaining!"
  • Waitlist starts: "First person added to waitlist."

üí° Perfect for:

Regular club games, casual meetups, open community events, recurring weekly games. Creates excitement as players watch the game fill up. Fair, transparent, and requires minimal management from you.

‚è≥

Waitlist Mode (Application-Based)

Complete control over player selection with private applications

üîÑ How Players Join

  1. 1
    Players click your link but cannot see who else applied - completely private process
  2. 2
    Everyone goes to your private waitlist (even if spots available) - no automatic confirmation
  3. 3
    You manually review all applications and strategically select players
  4. 4
    Selected players get promoted with one click - you control timing and selection

üëÄ What Players See

  • Game details only: Location, time, duration - no player information
  • Application notice: "The organizer will review applications and select players"
  • No visibility: Can't see who else applied or how many spots are left
  • Patient waiting: Players expect this to be a selection process

‚öôÔ∏è How You Manage It

Your management interface will typically show:

  • Confirmed Players: Just you initially (1/4 players)
  • Waitlist: All applicants waiting for your decision (4+ people)
  • Your role: Strategic team curation and selection
  • You control: Every promotion, timing, and decision

See detailed management examples in the "Managing Your Game" section ‚Üí

üéØ Strategic Selection Process

With Waitlist mode, you can:

  • Balance skill levels: Mix beginners with experienced players
  • Consider personalities: Choose players who work well together
  • Time your decisions: No rush - select when you're ready
  • Maintain privacy: Players can't see who else wants to play
  • Handle VIPs: Ensure certain people get priority

üì± Communication Strategy

Best practices for Waitlist mode:

  • Set expectations: Let players know selection may take time
  • Send updates: "Still reviewing applications - decisions by Friday"
  • Be selective: Players understand this is curated, not first-come
  • Communicate changes: If you need to adjust selection criteria

üí° Perfect for:

Competitive tournaments, skill-level specific games, private group events, exclusive gatherings, team balancing scenarios. Complete control over team composition and player selection with privacy.

ü§î Still Not Sure Which Mode?

Quick Decision Guide:

Choose First Come First Serve if: You want fairness, transparency, minimal management, and automatic flow

Choose Waitlist Mode if: You want control, privacy, strategic selection, and don't mind manual management

Remember: You can always try both modes with different games to see which works better for your group!

Game Setup and Creation

Questions about creating and configuring your games

üì± Which organizer notifications should I choose?

Recommended for beginners:
  • Game becomes full: Always helpful to know you're set
  • Someone cancels: Critical for finding last-minute replacements
Optional notifications:
  • Someone joins: Only if you like frequent updates (can be a lot of texts)
  • One spot left: Skip this if you already get "Someone joins"
  • Waitlist starts: Useful if you want to know when overflow begins

üë§ Should I include myself in the player count?

Yes, if you're playing, include yourself in the total. When you create the game, check "I will be playing in this game" and count yourself. For example, if you need 4 total players including yourself, set the player count to 4. You'll automatically be added as Player 1.

üìÖ Can I edit game details after creating?

Yes! You can edit most game details anytime:
  • Location and court number
  • Date and time
  • Duration
  • Number of players needed
  • Optional message
  • Your notification preferences
Cannot be changed: Registration mode (First Come First Serve vs Waitlist)

üîó How do I share my game with players?

After creating your game, you'll get a "Copy Invitation Message" button that creates a complete invitation including:
  • All game details (location, time, duration)
  • The registration link
  • Clear instructions for players
  • Mode-appropriate messaging
You can paste this complete message into texts, emails, social media, or any messaging app.

üèüÔ∏è What's the court number field for?

Use this to specify which court(s) at your location. Examples: "Court 3", "Courts 1-2", "Center Court", "Pickleball Courts A&B". This helps players find you at venues with multiple courts. It's optional but very helpful at larger facilities.

Game Management

Questions about managing players and running your games

üì± How do I get my management link?

Try these methods in order:
  1. 1
    Check "My Games": On the same device and browser where you created the game, click "My Games" in the navigation
  2. 2
    Text "1": Reply "1" to any SMS you received about your game - you'll get the management link instantly
  3. 3
    Contact IN or OUT: If the above don't work, reach out to IN or OUT support with your game details

üîÑ What's the difference between "Promote" and "Remove"?

Promote: Moves a player from waitlist to confirmed spots. They get an SMS saying they're now confirmed.

Remove: Completely deletes a player from the game. They get notified they've been removed and won't receive any more notifications about this game.

To Waitlist: Moves a confirmed player back to the waitlist (useful if you need to make room for someone else).

üë• Can I add players manually?

Yes! Use the "Add Player Manually" section in your management interface. This is perfect for:
  • Friends who don't use smartphones
  • Players who prefer not to use the online system
  • Last-minute additions you want to include
You can add them directly to confirmed spots or to the waitlist, with or without phone numbers.

üì¢ How do I send announcements to players?

Go to the "Communication" tab in your management interface. You can:
  • Send custom messages to all players, just confirmed players, or just waitlisted players
  • Use quick messages like game reminders or location details
  • Send updates about weather, court changes, or equipment needs
All messages go out as SMS and include your game details for context.

‚ùå What happens when I remove a player?

If you remove a confirmed player:
  1. They get an SMS saying they've been removed from the game
  2. If there's a waitlist, the first person automatically gets promoted
  3. The promoted person gets an SMS saying they're now confirmed
If you remove a waitlisted player: They just get notified of removal and everyone else moves up one position.

üö´ How do I cancel my entire game?

In your management interface, go to "Game Actions" tab and click "Cancel This Game". You can include a reason (like "Weather conditions" or "Court unavailable"). All players will automatically receive an SMS notification about the cancellation with your reason.

Troubleshooting

Solutions to common problems and technical issues

üò∞ I lost my management link! How do I get it back?

Don't panic! Try these steps in order:
  1. 1
    "My Games" page: On the same device and browser where you created the game, go to IN or OUT and click "My Games"
  2. 2
    Reply "1" to any SMS: Text "1" to any message you received about your game - instant management link
  3. 3
    Contact IN or OUT support: If both above fail, reach out with your game details (date, location, your phone number)
Pro tip: Once you get your link back, bookmark it or save it somewhere safe!

üì± Players aren't getting SMS messages - what's wrong?

Check these common issues:
  • Phone number format: Make sure numbers include area codes (555) 123-4567
  • International numbers: May not be supported depending on your location
  • Carrier blocking: Some carriers block automated messages - ask players to check spam
  • STOP commands: If a player previously replied "STOP", they won't get messages
Solution: Have players text "START" to re-enable SMS if they previously opted out.

‚è∞ I need to change the game time - what should I do?

Here's the best process:
  1. 1
    Edit game details: Update the time in your management interface
  2. 2
    Send announcement: Immediately notify all players about the time change
  3. 3
    Monitor responses: Watch for cancellations due to the new time
Note: Time changes often cause cancellations, so be prepared to promote from waitlist or find replacements.

ü§î Players are confused about the registration process - help!

For First Come First Serve: Emphasize that it's "first X are in" and they'll see who else is playing.

For Waitlist Mode: Clearly communicate that you'll be selecting players and include your timeline.

Common clarifications to include:
  • "Click the link to join - don't just reply to this message"
  • "You'll get a confirmation text when you register"
  • "Reply '2' to any text about the game for details"
  • "Reply '9' to any text to cancel your spot"

üåßÔ∏è Weather looks bad - should I cancel?

Consider your options:
  • Cancel proactively: If conditions will clearly be unplayable
  • Send weather update: "Game still on but monitoring weather - will update by [time]"
  • Move indoors: Edit location if you have backup courts
  • Postpone: Cancel this game and create a new one for a different date
Always communicate early and clearly - players appreciate advance notice rather than last-minute changes.
